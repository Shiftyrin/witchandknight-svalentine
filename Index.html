Once upon a time, in a world torn between light and darkness, there was a witch—feared by all, whispered in legends. She was said to be untouchable, a mistress of forbidden spells, the last of her kind. None who stood against her had ever walked away.
And then, there was the knight. A warrior draped in shadows, feared not because he fought against the darkness, but because he was the darkness itself. His blade had silenced every witch before her, his presence a storm upon the battlefield.
When their paths crossed, the witch smirked, her voice laced with defiance. "Your armor is nothing but metal. I will turn you into a frog before you lift your blade."
A crackling spell shot toward him—a burst of darkness strong enough to crumble mountains.
And yet, it did nothing.
The knight stood still, unfazed. His voice was calm, almost amused. "You studied the darkness? I was born from it."
For the first time in her life, the witch hesitated. Fear crept into her heart. "Then… we are on the same side?" she whispered. "Why would you slaughter your own? Why would you destroy those who walk the same path as you?"
His answer was cold. "Because I do not care. Light, darkness—none of it matters. Only one thing does. I must be the strongest. Even if it means standing alone."
The witch had no words. What could she say to a man who had already given up on the world?
She closed her eyes and surrendered to fate.
His blade rose, ready to sever the last thread of her life. But in that final moment, something happened.
The knight hesitated.
A force more powerful than magic, heavier than fate itself, rooted him in place. His hand trembled. His breath caught in his throat.
His eyes—once void of meaning—now saw her.
Her defiant gaze. Her wild, untamed spirit. The fire that still burned within her despite facing death.
"What is this power?" he whispered, his voice breaking.
He tried to look away, to fight it, but he couldn’t.
He dropped his sword.
Knees hitting the ground, he looked up at the witch, lost and undone.
And then, she did something neither of them expected. She knelt before him, her arms wrapping around him like the first warmth after a never-ending winter.
"I think... it's love."
The knight closed his eyes, letting himself sink into her embrace.
"Let’s leave it all behind," she whispered. "Light, darkness, war, blood—it’s all killing us. Let’s walk away from it together. Just you and me."
For the first time in his life, the knight smiled.
"Yes, Your Majesty."
From that moment, he no longer served the darkness nor the light. He was hers, and she was his.
They lived together. They died together.
